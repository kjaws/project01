my experience

What did you find challenging about this project?

The most challenging part for me was creating the responsive grid. In the intro level web design and development class, I did not learn Bootstrap, or any other grid system. I had issues combining the image and information within the same continer, considering many of the artist's names and titles of work were longer than the others. Once I decided I wanted to make each image a sqaure in an equal grid, and add a key at the bottom relating to each piece, it was much easier.

What did you find interesting about this project?

I actually didn't know you could validate your code to check for errors, so that was interesting. I enjoyed experimenting with opacity in the navigation bar and the main white background on each page. I wanted to display the background photos (all taken by me), yet still creating a clean, readable design.

Were there any techniques you learned by doing this project?

I learned a lot about that grid system, which still seems oddly more complicated than I'd expect it to be. Regardless, it's a good technique to know for laying out a page. I learned more about hover transitions on my art page, and I'm happy with the outcome of border and brightness transition. I also learned about the z-index: at one point my fixed navigation bar was hidden under my images on my art page when you scrolled down. Clearly I couldn't keep that, having some menu options unclickable and hidden. Such an easy fix!

How would you improve a portion of this project?

I'd love to improve my grid. I am satisfied with what I came up with, and although it is responsive when you resize the window, I couldn't figure out the breakpoints to shoot a 3x3 grid to become one multi-rowed column. It still works to an extent, but I'd like it better if that responsiveness was cleaner. I also had other ideas I would've liked to implement. For example, I thought of a left floating grid along a right floating key. Also when you'd hover over an image, the corresponding information would bold or highlight to make it clear which artist went with which work of art.
